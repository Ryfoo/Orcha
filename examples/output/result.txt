- Renewable energy, particularly solar and wind, has rapidly expanded in 2025, with over 600 gigawatts of new solar installations globally.
- Key regions like China, the EU, and the US have achieved national records in renewable energy capacity.
- Battery storage costs have dropped by approximately 90% since 2015, enabling better integration of renewable sources with demand.
- Some regional grids now operate on over 70% renewable energy for extended periods without needing to reduce output.
- Ongoing challenges include grid stability, mineral supply chain issues, and permitting delays.
- The 2020s are shaping up to be a critical decade where clean electricity becomes mainstream rather than niche.
